    Checking the validity of a probability function

    Use case: We are given some probability distribution (either a p.m.f. or a p.d.f.), and we are asked to confirm it is valid.

    Workflow: Check if the properties hold true.

    For a p.m.f. of a discrete rv

    1. \(\sum_x p(x) = 1\)
    2. \(p(x) \in (0,1],\)

    and for a p.d.f. of a continuous rv

    1. \(\int f(x) = 1\), over the range of \(x\)
    2. \(f(x) \in (0,1)\)

    Calculating a normalising constant

    Use case: We are given a c.d.f. \(F(x)\), and asked to calculate the normalising constant \(k\) so the c.d.f. sums to 1.

    Workflow:

    1. Solve \(k = \int_{a}^{b} f(x) \> dx\).

    Rationale:

    We know by the definition of the c.d.f. that

    \[ \begin{aligned} 1 = \int_{a}^{b} f(x) \> dx. \end{aligned} \]

    Therefore, if the p.d.f. is \(\frac{1}{k} f(x)\), then it must be that

    \[ \begin{aligned} 1 = \int_{a}^{b} \frac{1}{k} f(x) \> dx &= \frac{1}{k} \int_{a}^{b} f(x) \> dx \\ k &= \int_{a}^{b} f(x). \end{aligned} \]

    Calculating quantiles in the continuous case

    Use case: We are given a p.d.f. \(f(x)\) or c.d.f. \(F(x)\), and asked to calculate some \(\alpha-\)quantile of the distribution.

    Workflow:

    1. Find the c.d.f. It maybe given in the question, but it may not be.
    2. Identify \(\alpha \in (0,1)\).
    3. Solve \(q_{\alpha} = F^{-1}(\alpha)\). Rearrange \(F(q_{\alpha}) = \alpha\), so \(q_{\alpha}\) is the subject of the equation.

    Using standard probability models

    Use case: We are given a scenario, and asked to calculate the probability of some event.

    Workflow:

    1. Identify the distribution. It may be explicitly given in the question.
    2. Map the information to the parameters.
    3. Parse the probabilities.
    4. Solve the equation(s).

    Note, do not forget to state any approximation in the final statement.

    Poisson process

    Use case: We are given some process that occurs in real time with a rate \(\lambda\), and asked to identify the probability of some event occuring within some time interval.

    Workflow:

    1. Formalise the rate. Pay attention to the unit of time.
    2. Determine the rate \(\lambda t\) of the question. Is the time interval in question the same as that of that for the rate \(\lambda\)? If not, then scale \(\lambda\) by using \(\lambda t\)
    3. Parse the probability.
    4. For the number of events \(X\). Use \(X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda t)\)
    5. For the waiting time between events \(T\). Use \(T \sim M(\lambda)\) (do not use \(\lambda t\))
